Cross Direct Effects in Settings with Two Mediators

Erin E Gabriel, Arvid Sj√∂lander, Dean Follmann, Michael C Sachs
March 2022

Abstract

When multiple mediators are present, there are additional effects that may be of interest beyond the well-known natural and controlled direct effects. These effects cross the type of control on the mediators holding one to a constant level and one to its natural level, which differs across subjects, under some intervention. We introduce five such estimands for the cross controlled and natural direct effects when two mediators are measured and the intervention of interest is randomized. Such estimands may be of interest in immunology, as we discuss in relation to measured immunological responses to SARS-CoV-2 vaccination. We provide identifying expressions for the estimands in settings where there is no unmeasured confounding and the intervention, outcome and mediators are of arbitrary type. We further provide tight symbolic bounds for the estimands in settings where there may be unmeasured confounding and the intervention, mediators and outcome are binary.
